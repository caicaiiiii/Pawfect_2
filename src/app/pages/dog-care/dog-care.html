Dog care tips
for every owner

Caring for a dog means more than giving food and shelter. Dogs need proper nutrition, exercise, grooming, training, and regular health care to live happy and healthy lives.

Essential dog care
you should know

Proper feeding

Feed your dog balanced meals based on age, size, and activity level. Choose quality food and avoid overfeeding.

Fresh water

Make sure your dog always has access to clean drinking water throughout the day, especially after exercise.

Regular grooming

Brush your dog regularly, bathe when needed, and keep the coat, ears, nails, and teeth clean and healthy.

Daily exercise

Dogs need physical activity and playtime every day to stay fit, active, and mentally stimulated.

Training and safety

Use a secure collar or harness and practice leash training, basic commands, and safe outdoor habits.

Health care

Schedule regular vet visits, vaccinations, and parasite prevention to protect your dog from illness.

A simple daily
dog care routine

Morning

Start with food and water

Begin the day by checking your dog’s water bowl, serving breakfast, and taking them outside for a short walk or bathroom break.

Afternoon

Play and movement

Give your dog time for exercise, short training sessions, or interactive play to keep their body and mind active.

Evening

Clean, feed, and rest

Serve dinner, clean feeding tools, wipe paws if needed, and create a calm environment for rest and sleep.

Healthy habits
that make a difference

Feeding and hydration

Feed your dog on a regular schedule and choose food that fits their breed size, age, and activity level. Avoid giving unsafe human food, and always keep fresh water available.

Grooming and hygiene

Brush your dog’s coat, bathe only when needed, clean the ears carefully, trim the nails, and maintain dental hygiene for better comfort and health.

Exercise and play

Walks, games, and active play help dogs stay strong and happy. Exercise also reduces boredom and supports good behavior at home.

Vet visits and prevention

Regular checkups, vaccines, deworming, and flea or tick prevention help your dog stay protected and allow problems to be found early.
